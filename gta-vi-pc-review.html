Overview

Grand Theft Auto VI has finally arrived on PC, and after spending over 60 hours exploring Vice City and its surrounding areas, we can confidently say this is one of the most ambitious games ever created. Rockstar Games has outdone themselves in nearly every department — from the jaw-dropping visuals to the deeply complex open world that feels truly alive.

The game follows protagonists Jason and Lucia as they navigate the criminal underworld of a modern Vice City. The dual protagonist system works brilliantly, offering two distinct perspectives on the same story that complement each other perfectly.

💡 Quick Verdict: GTA VI is a generational leap in open-world gaming. It's not just a new GTA — it's a new standard for the entire industry.

Graphics & Visuals

On PC with a high-end system, GTA VI is absolutely breathtaking. The game uses Rockstar's new RAGE engine iteration that delivers photorealistic lighting, incredibly detailed character models, and a living, breathing world that reacts dynamically to weather, time of day, and player actions.

Ray tracing support transforms the nighttime streets of Vice City into something truly stunning. Reflections in puddles, neon signs bouncing off wet roads, and volumetric fog create an atmosphere that no other game has achieved. At 4K with DLSS 4, the game runs beautifully on modern hardware.

The open world itself is a technical marvel. Each NPC has their own daily routine, reacts to your behavior, and even talks on their phone about real events happening in the game world. The ocean simulation, vegetation system, and dynamic destruction are all best-in-class.

Gameplay & Story

GTA VI's story is the most mature and well-written in the series. The 40+ hour main campaign covers themes of loyalty, ambition, and survival with genuine emotional depth. Cutscenes are cinematic masterpieces, and the voice acting — particularly from the lead characters — is award-worthy.

Missions are more varied than ever, ranging from high-speed car chases to elaborate heists requiring careful planning. The new "Heat" system makes law enforcement feel genuinely dangerous rather than a minor inconvenience. Getting a five-star wanted level is now a true emergency situation.

The driving model has been completely overhauled with a physics system that makes every vehicle feel distinct and satisfying. From the floaty handling of classic muscle cars to the precision of supercars, driving in GTA VI is the best it has ever been.

PC Performance

Unlike GTA V's infamous PC launch, GTA VI arrives on PC in excellent shape. Optimization is solid across a wide range of hardware configurations. The game includes extensive graphics settings allowing you to fine-tune the experience for your specific system.

Recommended PC Specs for 1080p/60fps:

CPU: Intel Core i7-12700K or AMD Ryzen 7 5800X | GPU: NVIDIA RTX 3080 or AMD RX 6800 XT | RAM: 16GB DDR5 | Storage: 150GB NVMe SSD

NVIDIA DLSS 4 and AMD FSR 3 are both supported, providing significant performance boosts with minimal visual quality loss. At 1440p with DLSS Quality mode, even an RTX 3070 can maintain 60fps consistently.

✅ Pros

  • Stunning next-gen graphics
  • Massive, incredibly detailed open world
  • Excellent story with great characters
  • Best driving physics in the series
  • Well-optimized PC port
  • Hundreds of hours of content
  • GTA Online with major improvements

❌ Cons

  • Requires high-end hardware for max settings
  • Story ending feels slightly rushed
  • Some minor bugs at launch
  • Large file size (150GB+)

Final Verdict

GTA VI on PC is a landmark achievement in gaming. It pushes every boundary imaginable and delivers an experience that will be talked about for years. With a compelling story, stunning visuals, and endless content, this is an essential purchase for any PC gamer. Minor technical issues and the hefty hardware requirement keep it from perfection, but this is as close as gaming gets.

9.5 / 10
